J-pop Music

J-pop is a musical genre that has been a part of Japanese pop culture since the mid-twentieth century. J-pop applies to various music genres. It can also be heard in animes. The Bands that I like are Bump of Chicken, and Goose House (disbanded). Bump of Chicken has a nostalgia feel in their song, meanwhile Goose House has a happy feeling in the songs. Some J-pop were being used in games and anime which adds to my reason in liking it.

Video Game Music

Video Game music is a music composed for games using different kinds of music genre. It may not be categorized as a genre in music but I put video game music because I do not normally like the genre itself when it is not in games. Some video game music can be rock, pop, or orchestral. It depends on the game what kind of genre will be used. What I like with this music is that it has more feelings for me since some of the music is connected to the character of the game. Sometimes it tells the story of the character. I like some of the video game music because it makes me energetic, emotional, and excited.

Hip-Hop Music

A stylized rhythmic music originally created by DJs isolating the percussion breaks of popular songs, sometimes with two turntables to extend the breaks. Turntablist techniques, such as scratching, beat mixing/matching, and beat juggling eventually developed along with the breaks.
